ABOUT ME



Hello! My name is Dan Narcis Costinel and I am a third-year student at the Faculty of Automatic Control and Computer Science, part of the National University of Science and Technology Bucharest, specializing in Systems Engineering. I am passionate about programming, algorithms, and control systems, aiming to combine software logic with control theory to develop efficient solutions.


My interest in technical fields started in high school, where I studied Mathematics-Informatics. I discovered that programming and analytical thinking perfectly complement creative problem solving, motivating me to explore control systems, regulators, and numerical simulations.


Currently, my areas of interest include algorithms and data structures, optimization, machine learning, and automated systems. I have worked on diverse projects: complex software applications, regulator simulations, embedded systems, and games developed with Unity, involving both programming and mathematical control models.


I have volunteered as a teaching assistant for courses such as Algorithm Design and Object-Oriented Programming labs, guiding students in implementing data structures, graph algorithms, and understanding automated systems. This website represents my technical portfolio and showcases some of the projects I have worked on.
